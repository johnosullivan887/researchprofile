Semiconductor materials and interfaces

Experimental research in thin films, interfaces, and optoelectronic devices.

I am an experimental researcher working on semiconductor materials, interfaces, thin films, and optoelectronic devices. My work focuses on how material processing and interfacial design control charge transport, optical losses, recombination, and long-term stability in functional devices.

My background spans thin-film fabrication, electrical and optical characterisation, and device modelling. I am particularly interested in materials systems where performance is limited by interfaces and where careful experimental design can translate into scalable technologies.

Research

My research is centred on understanding how materials properties and interfaces determine the behaviour of semiconductor devices. This includes the interplay between conductivity, transparency, band alignment, recombination, and stability.

Transparent conductors

Graphene, ZnO, AZO, and related thin films for optoelectronic and energy applications.

Interfaces and transport

Energy alignment, contact resistance, interface passivation, and charge extraction.

Characterisation

Hall effect, Van der Pauw, UV-Vis, Kelvin probe, X-ray absorption spectroscopy, Raman, and device testing.

Modelling

Device-level simulations to connect measured material properties to performance limits.
