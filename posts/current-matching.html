Why current matching becomes much harder in triple-junction solar cells

One of the major challenges associated with monolithic triple-junction tandem solar cells is maintaining current matching between all three sub-cells. While adding additional junctions increases the theoretical efficiency limit, it also makes the device increasingly sensitive to optical losses, layer thicknesses, and parasitic absorption.
